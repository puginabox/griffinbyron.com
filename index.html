mobile development & design

Mobile design isn't tiny web design. It's not about making content look great on smaller screens. It is about delivering responsive content to users on the go, using the unique capabilities of mobile devices such as GPS and phone dialing to enhance the overall experience.

It's about seamlessly integrating content into users' daily lives -- connecting them socially, anticipating their needs, and creating dynamic, meaningful interaction.

flat-design

Simplicity is golden! In the chaos of information and media overload, users need crisp, simple interfaces that are intuitive, uncluttered, and responsive to their needs.

campy design

Design is supposed to be fun! Users want ready access to information, but also look for that whimsical touch that keeps them engaged, amused, and looking for more.

slick design

Detail matters! I strive to create polished, integrated designs that present a unified message from soup to nuts – through color, graphic elements, movement, and information architecture.

process

UX strategy

User-centered research methods allow for a focus on the client's target group, where all deliverables are functional and beautiful but show innovative and highly gratifying experiences.

research

Signifiers: are there any symbols, datasets or touch points that are relevant/can be tapped into? How can the interface become more transparent, intuitive, accessible, adaptive and content-driven? How can we give the user a unique experience?

who I am and what I do

I am a Pittsburgh-based multimedia artist and designer for print and media. I love design. I love how life influences design. I love how design influences life. I also really enjoy working with cool people to create really, really amazing things.

I like to think that I craft things that are meaningful and beautiful. A restless creativist who believes curiosity and hard work are the way to make things that matter. I'm not a rockstar. I'm not a ninja. I'm just a guy that loves to make things that can have a real and lasting impact in people's lives.

the way I work

I strive to exceed client expectations in a collaborative environment. I have had many, many wonderful experiences working with art directors, media peoples, account executives, developers, copywriters and local artists. I am a self-styled mac-virtuoso, IT conqueror and casanova of all things pixel-perfect.

I believe in designing responsibly. Mobile-first, I am fluent in DHTML, with sparse, clean and crisp semantic markup, CSS3 (Sass/Compass, CSS Transitions), JSON, Javascript & Jquery. Progressive Enhancement is the new rad. Minification is Maximization and lean protocol requesting makes for firm abs. Agile Scrum frameworks, such as Ontime, or Rally, are the way to hang.

I am software-agnostic. I use what’s available, when available. My home dev environment includes Sublime Text 3, iTerm and Grunt, Emmet for speed, Git for getting it safe, Sass, Compass & Susy for responsive pre-processing goodness, all heave-hoed by Grunt. For all things design, I use Adobe CC.

I have a love-hate relationship with 3D. I love making it, seeing others making it, absorbing it through all channels, but hate that i have no time to perfect my skills in it. I use Maya, Zbrush, Mudbox, & Sculptris for modeling, texturing, UV mapping, basic Fluid Dynamics and linear/non-linear animation.

I love to mock up. Wireframes, interactive prototypes, presentations to the masses, communicating storyboards, these all make me tick. Aside from using Abobe CC, Keynote & Powerpoint (if I have to), I also use online tools such as Mockingbird and Balsamiq. And no, I do not use Prezi. It makes me seasick.

Slick animations turn me on. I use Adobe Edge, After Effects, Flash (if I have to) and Maya to make ad banners. I have, in the past, used DoubleClick for agency digital ad management.
