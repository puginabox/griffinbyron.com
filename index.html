mobile development & design

Mobile design isn't tiny web design. It's not about making content look great on smaller screens. It is about delivering responsive content to users on the go, using the unique capabilities of mobile devices such as GPS and phone dialing to enhance the overall experience.

It's about seamlessly integrating content into users' daily lives -- connecting them socially, anticipating their needs, and creating dynamic, meaningful interaction.

animation

Slick animations turn me on. I have been animating over the last 12 years, using predominately After Effects and Flash. In addition to these, I now tend to use Edge Animate, Maya, Blender, HTML5 Canvas, Processing and openFrameworks. What I use naturally depends on what the gig is.

For 3D & video, I have been leaning towards Blender, ever since I ceased being a student and can't get Maya for free. For video (not that I have done much, to date) I use Premiere and After Effects. For real-time openGL processing, I tend to use Max 6 (Jitter), openProcessing, Quartz Composer and, to a lesser extent, Processing. For web-based stuff, like agency ads, banners etc, I use Edge Animate, Createjs, Easeljs, Canvas and CSS Transitions. The web is still in a process of maturation, and until a new standard arises ubiquitous for moving vector/non-encapsulated moving graphics, I tend more towards Edge, even though it spits out bloated un-semantic code.

the way I work

I strive to exceed client expectations in a collaborative environment. I have had many, many wonderful experiences working with art directors, media peoples, account executives, developers, copywriters and local artists. I am a self-styled mac-virtuoso, IT conqueror and casanova of all things pixel-perfect.

I believe in designing responsibly. Mobile-first, I am fluent in DHTML, with sparse, clean and crisp semantic markup, CSS3 (Sass/Compass, CSS Transitions), JSON, Javascript & Jquery. Progressive Enhancement is the new rad. Minification is Maximization and lean protocol requesting makes for firm abs. Agile Scrum frameworks, such as Ontime, or Rally, are the way to hang.

In terms of a CMS, well thats simple. Drupal is King. From content management and database setup, backup and maintenance, through to the creation of custom-made subthemes and module development. I love to own a full web stack, where front-end, mid and backend stages in development give me the ability to enter any stage of the complete process from staging environments to production. I am a HTML, CSS and Javascript specialist, and am fluent with modern marketing techniques using Marketing code tracking within platforms such as Sparkroom and ION Liveball.

I am software-agnostic. I use what’s available, when available. My home dev environment includes Sublime Text 3, iTerm, SpriteRight for sprites, Bower for keeping it current, Emmet for speed, Git for getting it safe, Sass, Compass & Susy for responsive pre-processing goodness, all heave-hoed by Grunt. MVC is the way to go, and I have just recently started using backbone.js, with Mustache/Handlebars, along with Testem/Jasmine for testing it all. For all things design, I use Adobe CC, though I have been leaning towards Blender and Inksape, being freebies and all.

I have a love-hate relationship with 3D. I love making it, seeing others making it, absorbing it through all channels, but hate that i have no time to perfect my skills in it. I used to use Maya, Zbrush and Mudbox during my student years, but opt for opensource out in the real world. So these days I use Blender & Sculptris for modeling, texturing, UV mapping, basic Fluid Dynamics and linear/non-linear animation.

I love to mock up. Wireframes, interactive prototypes, presentations to the masses, communicating storyboards, these all make me tick. Aside from using Abobe CC, Keynote & Powerpoint (if I have to), I also use online tools such as Mockingbird and Balsamiq. And no, I do not use Prezi. It makes me seasick.

Slick animations turn me on. I have been animating over the last 12 years, using After Effects and Flash. In addition to these, I now tend to use Edge Animate, Maya, HTML5 Canvas and Processing. This depends on what the gig is, of course. I am hoping to check outD3.js, the new kid on the block, hopefully pretty soon. I have, in the past, used DoubleClick for agency digital ad management.

Lately, I have been hanging out at Techshop, and for laser cutting, I use Illustrator, for CNC/CAD/CAM/Fabrication, I use AutoCAD, VCarve Pro and InventorCAM, often starting sometimes in Blender. For embedded fun, prefer programming PIC chips but also play around with Arduino. Check out my Instructables, to see recent projects!

process

Each project as its own truly individual story. Every project is a good one, no matter what its size, schedule, budget or subject.

Tolleson Design, in "Soak, Wash, Rinse, Spin"

My process differs greatly, depending on the gig, project, timeline, scope and people. Generally, I believe in mockups occurring around the same time as deciphering grphaical/look elements, and thus, tend to abstain from wireframing. It i easier to get a sense of a look and feel in these early stages is mockups have color, style and other visual elements. That way, refinement of the assets can take on precision at the same stage as blocking out content.

I love to be a key player responisible for deilverables from initial ideation, design mocks, before tackling marketing direction and implementation through to production launch. I love being a part of a complete design lifecycle - from discussions with vendors, team assessments and dissemination of tasks, before the conception, creation and execution of the “look and feel” of online digitals and all marketing and branding materials.

UX strategy

research
